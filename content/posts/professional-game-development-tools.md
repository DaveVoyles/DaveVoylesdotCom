| [Construct 2](https://www.scirra.com/) | [construct-2-logo](/images/www.davevoyles.com_wp-content_uploads_2014_03_construct-2-logo.jpg.jpg) |

*“I want to get started with making games, but I don’t know how. Where should I start?”*

It can be overwhelming when you are starting off, because there are so many options available to you. I mean just look at plethora of game making tools at  [Pixel Prospetor](http://www.pixelprospector.com/the-big-list-of-game-making-tools/), and you’ll see what I’m talking about.  If you have zero programming experience, then I’d suggest starting with [Construct2.](http://www.pixelprospector.com/the-big-list-of-game-making-tools/#construct)

Construct 2 allows you to create HTML5 games without ever having to write code. Everything is handled in the IDE (Integrated Development Environment) which has a GUI (Graphical User Interface) that anyone could use. You simply drag-and-drop objects into your scene, and can make changes to the objects properties by right clicking.

Even better, it’s free! There are premium versions available, but if you’re just starting off, then the free version offers more than enough to get you going.

Moreover, there are tons of resources to take advantage of:

* [Construct Tutorials](http://www.scirra.com/help/)
* [Construct Wiki](http://www.scirra.com/help/)
* [Construct Demos](http://www.scirra.com/construct/demos.php)

I’d suggest starting here, though:

* [Install Construct2](https://www.scirra.com/construct2/releases/r158.2)
* [[TUTORIAL] How to make a platformer game](https://www.scirra.com/tutorials/253/how-to-make-a-platform-game/page-1#tuttop)
* MVA – [Construct 2 Advanced Game Development](http://www.microsoftvirtualacademy.com/training-courses/construct-2-advanced-game-development)

| [Unreal Engine 4](https://www.unrealengine.com/blog/welcome-to-unreal-engine-4) | [unrealengine4logo](http://davevoyles.azurewebsites.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/unrealengine4logo.png) |

Fresh off of the Game Developers Conference, Epic revealed that their newest engine would be made available to everyone immediately, for free. This is a big change from the previous licensing agreement of UDK.

A complete revamp of the UI has taken place, in addition to a new visual scripting system called “Blueprints”. Gone are the days of UnrealScript as well, as of the engine’s programming is now done in C++. Even better: the [entire source code for the engine is available for free.](https://www.unrealengine.com/ue4-on-github) The editor works on Mac and PC and projects can be deployed for Windows, OSX, iOS, and Android, in addition to the PS4 and Xbox One.

| [Unreal Development Kit (UDK)](http://www.unrealengine.com/udk/) | [UDK_LOGO_banner](http://davevoyles.azurewebsites.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/UDK_LOGO_banner.png) |

UDK makes use of its own programming language, called [UnrealScript,](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UnrealScript) which may throw some new developers through a loop. The language was designed for simple, high-level programming langage. The UnrealScript interpreter was programmed by[Tim Sweeney](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tim_Sweeney_(game_developer) "Tim Sweeney (game developer)"), who continues to develop the newest version of the engine, Unreal Engine 4.

Similar to [Java](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Java_(programming_language) "Java (programming language)"), UnrealScript is [object-oriented](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Object-oriented "Object-oriented") without [multiple inheritance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_inheritance "Multiple inheritance") (classes all inherit from a common Object class), and classes are defined in individual files named for the class they define. This author j[ust happens to have written a book](http://www.packtpub.com/unrealscript-game-programming-cookbook/book#overview) about how to program for this engine. Many of last generation’s most popular titles used this engine, including Gears of War, Mass Effect, and Bioshock.

| [BabylonJS](http://www.babylonjs.com/) | [BabylonJS-logo](/images/www.davevoyles.com_wp-content_uploads_2014_03_BabylonJS-logo.png.png) |

WebGL allows your web browser to talk to the GPU on your device, whether it is a desktop machine or a phone. Writing low level code to allow for this interaction can be a bit of a barrier to some, so several engineers from Microsoft have created the free and open source framework, BabylonJS.

You can easily write JavaScript to create high-powered experiences inside of the browser, or wrap it with a third party tool like Cordova or the Windows 10 UWP to allow it to run as though it were a native application on your device and sell it in the app store of your choice. [Take a look at the playground](http://www.babylonjs-playground.com/) to get a better feel for how it all works. It also supports Oculus Rift, the gamepad API, and you can easily export 3D assets from tools like Blender to have them work in your browser!

| [Autodesk Maya](http://www.autodesk.com/products/autodesk-maya/overview) / [LT](http://www.gamasutra.com/view/news/199094/Maya_LT_is_Autodesks_new_indie_and_mobilefocused_modeling_tool.php) | [Maya Logo](http://davevoyles.azurewebsites.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Maya-Logo.png) |

This application used to generate 3D assets for use in film, television, game development and architecture. There are a number of new licensing terms as of late, and a perpetual student license can be purchased for Maya. This license does not expire and the student version can be upgraded to the commercial version at a significant discount. Even better, after the student graduates, the only restriction being non commercial use

One large benefit in Maya’s corner however, is the fact that it works on both PCs and Macs. Due to Autodesk’s acquisition of Maya in 2005, the differences between Maya and Max have begun to converge, so it’s largely a matter of personal preference at this point. While the differences are subtle, larger studios tend to stick with one tool or the other.

At Unite 2013, Unity’s annual gaming conference, Maya announced that they are now releasing a lighter version of Maya for indies called [MayaLT](http://www.gamasutra.com/view/news/199094/Maya_LT_is_Autodesks_new_indie_and_mobilefocused_modeling_tool.php), which can be purchased using a variety of subscription models. Prices start at $50 / month, and is perfect for those looking for a short term licencing model.

[![Maya 2014](http://davevoyles.azurewebsites.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Maya-2014.jpg)](http://davevoyles.azurewebsites.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Maya-2014.jpg)

| [Autodesk 3DS Max](http://www.autodesk.com/products/autodesk-3ds-max/overview) | [3ds max ogo](http://davevoyles.azurewebsites.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/3ds-max-ogo.png) |

3DS Max is perhaps slightly more popular in the gaming industry, but you shouldn’t have a problem finding a job if you know Maya either. The hotkeys are very different and the viewports may throw users off when coming from one tool to the other, but they largely perform the same tasks.

Many game engines feature workflows for easy exporting from MAX to the particular engine, so look carefully before making your decision about what to purchase, and gauge the value of the tool from there.

Alternatively, [Blender](http://www.blender.org/) is an open source and free 3D modeling tool, but not nearly as featured as either of Autodesks offerings. Considering that most professionals use the two tools listed above, I’d suggest skipping over Blender completely.

| [Audition](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adobe_Audition) | [Adobe Audition](http://davevoyles.azurewebsites.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Adobe-Audition.png) |

In terms of audio tools, Adobe *Audition*, along with Avid *Pro* Tools, are the defacto standard. You can now purchase it as part of Adobe’s Creative Cloud suite, which include Photoshop, Illustrator, and other image editing tools.  Alternatively, [*Audacity*](http://audacity.sourceforge.net/) is a free audio editing tool which works for those of us on a cheaper budget.

Even if you weren’t using this for soundtracks or sound effects for games, it makes for an excellent tool for creating and editing podcasts, which are invaluable when it comes to promoting your work.
